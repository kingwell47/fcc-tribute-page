Satoru Iwata

Nintendo President 2002-2015

Here are some notable years in Mr. Iwata's life and career:

  • 1959 - Born in Sapporo, Japan.
  • 1978 - Admitted to the Tokyo Institute for Technology, where he majored in computer science.
  • 1982 - Started working full-time for HAL Laboratory (HAL), after previously working there part-time while studying.
  • 1983 - Became coordinator of software production for HAL, where he helped create a relationship with Nintendo to be able to produce games for the Nintendo Entertainment System (NES). He worked on the NES port for the arcade game Joust, as well as games such as Balloon Fight, NES Open Tournament Golf, Earthbound and the Kirby games. His work on NES Open Tournament Golf is particularly noteworthy as Iwata had to create his own data compression method in order to fit all 18 courses into the game, a feat that Nintendo did not believe was possible at that time.
  • 1993 - Promoted to president of HAL at the insistence of Nintendo's then-President, Hiroshi Yamauchi. With assistance from Nintendo, Iwata helped turn the company around from its ¥1.5 billion debt and stabilize its finances within six years.
  • 1999 - Assisted in the development of Pokémon Gold and Silver for the Game Boy Color, by creating a set of compression tools utilized for graphics in the games. According to Tsunekazu Ishihara, the president of The Pokémon Company, Iwata was instrumental in bringing Pokémon to Western markets.
  • 2000 - Joined Nintendo as the head of its corporate planning division and took a seat on the board of directors. Over the next two years, he sought to reduce the cost and length of game development while preserving quality.
  • 2002 - Succeeded as Nintendo's fourth president with Hiroshi Yamauchi's blessing. He was the first Nintendo president unrelated to the Yamauchi family through blood or marriage since its founding in 1889.
  • 2004 - Helped lead a revitalization of Nintendo's handheld business by transitioning the company from the Game Boy Advance to the Nintendo DS. The Nintendo DS proved to be a highly profitable system and went on to become the second-best selling video game console of all time with more than 154 million units, inclusive of subsequent iterations, sold by September 2014.
  • 2006 - Nintendo launches the Nintendo Wii. The controller's remote control design partially stemmed from Iwata's desire to have a device that was "immediately accessible" to all. He also insisted that the Wii Remote be referred to as simply a "remote" rather than a controller to emphasize its accessibility to anyone. The Wii ultimately popularized the use of motion control-based video games and proved highly successful for Nintendo, helping to nearly double the company's stock price.
  • 2010 - Subsequent hardware units under Iwata's tenure, including the Nintendo 3DS and Wii U, were not as successful as the DS and Wii, and Nintendo's finances took a downward turn. Iwata voluntarily halves his salary in 2011 and 2014 as apologies for the poor sales while other members of the Nintendo board of directors had pay cuts of 20 to 30 percent.
  • 2015 - Was hospitalized and ultimately dies due to complications from a bile duct tumor at the age of 55. Despite his hospitalization, Iwata continued to work via his laptop in his bed and provided feedback on Pokémon Go.
  • 2017 - Nintendo launches the Nintendo Switch, where Iwata served as the console's head developer during the final months of his life. As of June 30, 2022, Nintendo has shipped 111.08 million Switch units, with over 84.77 million original Switch units, 18.99 million Switch Lite units, and 7.32 million Switch OLED units. With these numbers the Switch has surpassed lifetime sales of the Wii U (13.6 million units), GameCube (21.7 million units), Nintendo 64 (32.9 million units), Super Nintendo Entertainment System (49.1 million units), Nintendo Entertainment System (61.9 million units), Nintendo 3DS (75.92 million units), Game Boy Advance (81.51 million units), and Wii (101.63 million units).
